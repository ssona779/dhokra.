About Dhokra Craft

Dhokra is an ancient art form of metal casting practiced by tribal artisans in India for over 4,000 years. Known for its unique wax technique and rustic beauty, Dhokra art features motifs of animals, deities, and tribal life — each piece carrying the soul of tradition and storytelling.

With no two pieces exactly alike, Dhokra art is a celebration of heritage, handcrafted with passion, patience, and age-old wisdom.

The Dhokra Craft Process

1. Wax Modeling

Artisans shape beeswax into intricate forms, creating the base design of the piece.

2. Clay Molding

Clay is layered over the wax model, forming a solid mold after drying.

3. Metal Pouring

Molten brass is poured into the mold, replacing the wax and forming the artifact.

4. Finishing

The mold is broken to reveal the metal piece, which is then polished and detailed.
